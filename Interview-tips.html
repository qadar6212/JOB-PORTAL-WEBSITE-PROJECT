Research Thoroughly

Before any interview, research is key to success:

  • Study the company's mission, values, and culture
  • Understand the company's products/services
  • Research your interviewers on LinkedIn
  • Know the industry trends and challenges

Dress Appropriately

First impressions matter. Dress for success:

  • Research the company dress code
  • When in doubt, business professional is safe
  • Ensure clothes are clean and well-fitted
  • Pay attention to grooming details

Communication Skills

How you communicate is as important as what you say:

  • Practice your elevator pitch
  • Use the STAR method for behavioral questions
  • Maintain eye contact and confident posture
  • Listen actively before responding

Prepare Questions

Asking thoughtful questions shows genuine interest:

  • Prepare 5-7 questions about the role/team
  • Ask about company culture and growth
  • Inquire about challenges and expectations
  • Avoid questions about salary/benefits early on

Common Interview Questions

"Tell me about yourself"

Keep it professional and relevant. Focus on your career journey, key skills, and why you're interested in this role. Limit to 2-3 minutes.

"What are your strengths and weaknesses?"

For strengths: Choose 3-4 relevant to the job. For weaknesses: Be honest but show you're working on improvement.

"Why do you want to work here?"

Connect your values with the company's mission. Mention specific aspects that attracted you to the organization.

"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

Show ambition but also commitment. Focus on skill development and contributions to the company's success.

Interview Day Checklist

  • Research the company thoroughly
  • Practice common interview questions
  • Prepare your interview outfit
  • Print extra copies of your resume
  • Plan your route and arrival time
  • Charge your phone and have contacts ready
  • Prepare questions to ask the interviewer
